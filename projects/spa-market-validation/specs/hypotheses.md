# Validation Hypotheses

Falsifiable assumptions for the spa owner market validation program. Mark each **validate**, **invalidate**, or **inconclusive** after M4 synthesis.

## H1 — Social content freshness is a real pain

**Statement:** Nail salon owners struggle to keep their social profiles fresh and on-trend while running day-to-day operations.

**How we test:** Ask pain rating (1–5) in section C of the interview; probe for time, inspiration, and consistency blockers in section B.

**Success signal:** ≥70% rate pain as 4 or 5 out of 5; majority cite time, inspiration, or posting consistency.

**Failure signal:** Pain rated ≤2 by majority; owners report social is "easy" or "not important for bookings."

## H2 — Trend research + post ideas improve workflow materially

**Statement:** A weekly trend briefing plus ready-to-edit post suggestions would save meaningful time or increase posting frequency.

**How we test:** Demo the product; ask "would you use a weekly trend briefing?"; estimate hours saved or posting frequency change.

**Success signal:** ≥60% say it would save ≥2 hours/week OR would post more often (e.g. weekly → 3×/week).

**Failure signal:** Owners say they already have enough inspiration or wouldn't change behavior.

## H3 — Trend discovery is informal, not tool-driven

**Statement:** Owners discover trends through scrolling social feeds, peers, and Pinterest — not through dedicated research or SaaS tools.

**How we test:** Section B workflow questions; document step-by-step how they chose their last promoted design.

**Success signal:** Current workflow documented in ≥80% of interviews; primary sources are IG/TikTok/Pinterest/peers, not structured tools.

**Failure signal:** Majority already use paid tools or agencies for trend research.

## H4 — Willingness to pay exists at SaaS price points

**Statement:** Owners will pay a recurring monthly fee for trend research and content assistance at levels that support a viable business.

**How we test:** Van Westendorp four-question pricing (section E); pilot likelihood score (0–10).

**Success signal:** "Acceptable" price band (between "bargain" and "expensive") overlaps across regions and aligns with starter/pro tiers in [pricing-summary.md](../outcomes/pricing-summary.md).

**Failure signal:** "Too expensive" threshold falls below operational cost; majority pilot score &lt;5.

## H5 — AI vs UGC preference varies by geography and post type

**Statement:** Comfort with AI-generated content differs by region and content type (captions vs images vs hashtags).

**How we test:** Content preference ranking cards (section D); likelihood to publish AI-assisted content (1–5).

**Success signal:** Clear per-region pattern (e.g. captions AI-ok globally, images owner-only in VN/FI).

**Failure signal:** No consistent pattern; individual variance dominates geography.
